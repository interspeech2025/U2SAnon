U2SAnon: A Uniform to Speaker Vector Anonymizer in Voice Anonymization

Abstract: Voice anonymization has emerged as a crucial technique for protecting speaker privacy in the era of advancing speech technologies. While existing methods for pseudo-speaker generation have shown promise, they often face challenges in computational efficiency and speaker distinctiveness. This paper presents U2SAnon, a novel approach to voice anonymization that addresses these limitations through efficient pseudo-speaker generation. Unlike conventional methods that operate directly in the speaker vector space or rely on reference pools, our proposed method samples pseudo-speaker identities from a uniform vector space and maps them to the speaker vector space. This approach significantly reduces computational complexity by eliminating the need for iterative sampling or distribution estimation while maintaining speaker distinctiveness. U2SAnon consists of two key components: a uniform identity vector sampler and a mapper that transforms vectors from the uniform space to the pseudo-speaker vector space. The system preserves the original speech's linguistic content and prosody while effectively anonymizing speaker identity. Experimental results on the LibriSpeech dataset demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed approach in terms of both computational efficiency and anonymization performance.
